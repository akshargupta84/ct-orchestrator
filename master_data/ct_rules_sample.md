# Creative Testing Rules & Guidelines

**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** January 2025  
**Last Updated:** January 2025

---

## 1. Overview

All campaigns must undergo creative testing before launch to ensure optimal creative performance. This document outlines the rules, limits, and processes for creative testing.

## 2. Budget Allocation

All campaigns must allocate **1.5%** of their total media budget to the creative testing pool.

**Example:**
- $10,000,000 campaign budget → $150,000 testing budget
- $50,000,000 campaign budget → $750,000 testing budget

## 3. Testing Limits by Budget Tier

The number of creatives that can be tested depends on the campaign budget:

| Campaign Budget | Video Assets | Display Assets | Audio Assets |
|-----------------|--------------|----------------|--------------|
| Less than $5M | 2 | 5 | 2 |
| $5M - $35M | 8 | 15 | 5 |
| $35M - $100M | 15 | 30 | 10 |
| Over $100M | 25 | 50 | 15 |

## 6. Minimum Requirements

### Mandatory Testing
- All campaigns over **$2,000,000** must test at least **1 video asset**
- Campaigns with TV spend must include TV creative in testing
- New creative concepts must be tested before full-scale deployment

### Sample Sizes
- Minimum sample size per cell: 1,000 respondents
- Recommended sample size: 1,500 respondents for 90% confidence
- Maximum cells per study: 6 (1 control + 5 exposed)

## 7. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

### Primary KPIs (must select one)
- **Awareness** - Top-of-mind and aided awareness
- **Consideration** - Purchase consideration intent
- **Preference** - Brand preference vs. competitors
- **Purchase Intent** - Likelihood to purchase

### Secondary KPIs (up to 3)
- Ad recall
- Message association
- Brand favorability
- Emotional response

### Statistical Significance
- Results must achieve **90% confidence** to be considered statistically significant
- Lift of **3% or higher** recommended for meaningful impact

## 8. Diagnostic Metrics

All tests include the following diagnostic metrics:

| Metric | Description | Benchmark |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Brand Strength | How strongly the ad connects to the brand | 65 |
| Relevance | How relevant the message is to the audience | 60 |
| Emotional Engagement | Emotional response to the creative | 55 |
| Uniqueness | How differentiated the creative is | 50 |
| Credibility | How believable the claims are | 65 |
| Call-to-Action Clarity | How clear the desired action is | 70 |
| Brand Fit | How well the creative fits the brand | 65 |
| Message Clarity | How clear the main message is | 70 |
| Likability | How much viewers enjoy the creative | 60 |
| Memorability | How memorable the creative is | 55 |

## 9. Testing Process

### Step 1: Plan Submission
1. Submit campaign details (budget, dates, KPIs)
2. Submit creative trix (creative × channel matrix)
3. Specify hypotheses to test
4. System validates against rules

### Step 2: Plan Approval
1. Review generated test plan
2. Request revisions if needed
3. Approve final plan
4. Creatives sent to testing vendor

### Step 3: Testing Execution
1. Vendor sets up study
2. Data collection period
3. Initial quality checks
4. Results compilation

### Step 4: Results Analysis
1. Results CSV uploaded to system
2. Automatic analysis and recommendations
3. Human review of insights
4. Stakeholder presentation

## 10. Recommendations Framework

### Pass Criteria
Creative **PASSES** if:
- Shows statistically significant lift (90% confidence)
- Primary KPI lift is positive
- No major diagnostic red flags

### Fail Criteria
Creative **FAILS** if:
- Does not achieve statistical significance
- Shows negative or no lift in primary KPI
- Multiple diagnostic metrics below benchmark

### Optimization Candidates
Creative is **OPTIMIZE & RETEST** if:
- Shows positive but non-significant lift
- Strong diagnostics but weak KPI lift
- Addressable issues identified
